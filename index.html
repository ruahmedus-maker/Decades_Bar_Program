Standard Operating Procedures

Consistency is key to our success. Follow these procedures to maintain our high standards and a clean work place.

Opening Checklist

  1. Arrive ready for your shift and clock it at the start time indicated on your schedule
  2. Collect banks and check-books (HH & Rooftop floors) from the office and confirm registers are assigned
  3. Remove all caps from liquor bottles and soak in soda water
  4. Set up the well - marry bottles and gather the liquor required for the shift
  5. Ensure all necessary tools, shakers, jiggers, etc are present
  6. Straight face all bottles on the back-bar display and fill any empty spaces with the appropriate bottles
  7. Review daily specials/promotions and make sure the specials menus are displayed on the bar

Closing Procedures

  1. Do not start entering tips into the POS system or start any other closing duties until the lights go up and we are no longer serving customers
  2. Soak bar guns in hot water (where available) or soda water to disolve residue
  3. Enter tips, change out cash tips with the drawer (giving the low denomination bills to the house in exchange for larger bills for yourselves), count the bank to $300. Straight face the remaining cash in the drawer (this will be the blind drop). Once you are done with those procedures please exit out of your screen so that management can run the report.
  4. Remove all bottles from the well and clean all steel surfaces with a wet rag and cleaning solution
  5. Clean all bottles in the same fashion and restore to the well
  6. Soak up standing water from the resevoir surrounding the ice bin
  7. Break down bar guns and clean thoroughly then place into holder
  8. Return any chilled bottles that may have been in your well back into the coolers
  9. Cap all bottles including those in the coolers
  10. Visually inspect the bar for any oversight
  11. Proceed to office for cash out

üí≥ Order Processing

New Order - Single Guest
  1. Select items from menu categories
  2. Add modifiers if needed (rocks, neat, etc.)
  3. Press "Send Order" to finalize
  4. Select payment method:
    • Cash - enter amount tendered
    • Credit - swipe/insert card
    • Room Charge - enter room number
    • Gift Card - swipe card
  5. Print receipt if requested
Tab Management
  1. Start tab by selecting "Hold Check"
  2. Assign to table number or customer name
  3. Add drinks throughout the night
  4. To close: Find tab in "Open Checks"
  5. Process payment and print final receipt
üí° Speed Tips
  • Use quick keys for popular drinks during rush
  • Start tabs for groups of 3+ automatically
  • Pre-authorize credit cards for large tabs
  • Use "Repeat Order" for multiple same drinks

üìã Check Management

Splitting Checks
  1. Open the check to be split
  2. Select "Split Check" option
  3. Choose split method:
    • Evenly: Divide total by number of ways
    • By Item: Assign items to different checks
    • By Amount: Specify exact amounts
  4. Process each split check separately
Transferring Checks
  1. Open the check to transfer
  2. Select "Transfer Check"
  3. Choose destination:
    • Another bartender station
    • Table service section
    • Bottle service area
  4. Confirm transfer with receiving employee

Glassware Guide

Decades Large Branded Glass

Primary Use: High-volume cocktails and mixed drinks

Includes:
  • Water, Juice, and Soda
  • Any Long Island Iced Tea variations
  • Any cocktail with Red Bull
  • Bombs for Red Bull
  • Decades Signature/Seasonal feature cocktails
  • Any "double" cocktail

Note: May be used as a double shooter upon specific customer request

Decades Frosted Glass

Primary Use: Spirit-forward drinks served without large-volume mixers

Includes:
  • Single or double "neat" pours of any liquor
  • Single-pour cocktails (e.g., Vodka & Soda, Rum & Coke)
  • Single or double pours of liquor "on the rocks"
  • Any request for a Neat/Straight Up shot (e.g., Bourbon Neat, Scotch Straight Up)

Decades Shot Glass

Primary Use: All shot servings

Includes:
  • Shots of liquor
  • All shooters

Standard: All shots are to be poured below the rim

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about procedures, policies, and best practices.

What should I do if a customer appears intoxicated?

Stop serving alcohol to the customer immediately. Offer water instead. Notify Security and Management.

How do I handle a spill?

If spill takes place infront of you at the bar, clean the area and replace the customers beverage. Start a Spill Tab and ring in the beverage you replaced. Note: You may not replace drinks for customers that arrive at the bar claiming to have spilled their drink in your absense

What's the procedure for processing a return?

All returns must be approved by a manager. Process the return through the POS system using the original payment method when possible. Document the reason for the return in the system notes.
